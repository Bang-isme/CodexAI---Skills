# Decision Contract

## Purpose
The decision layer prevents shallow single-path answers and overthinking. It does not ask the model to expose hidden reasoning. It asks for a compact, auditable decision surface.

## Required Fields
- Options: 2-4 credible choices.
- Evidence: repo files, commands, user constraints, or missing proof.
- Cost: implementation, operational, maintenance, and token cost.
- Risk: what breaks or becomes harder.
- Reversibility: how hard it is to undo.
- Verification: exact command or observable signal.
- Recommendation: one chosen direction with a reason.

## Anti-Token-Waste Policy
- No "million ideas" lists.
- No generic best-practice paragraphs.
- No repeated framing.
- No hidden chain-of-thought transcript.
- Prefer one compact table plus a recommendation.
